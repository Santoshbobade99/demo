What is IPO?

An Initial Public Offering (IPO) is the process through which a private company becomes a public company by offering its shares to the general public for the first time.
This process involves several key details and stages:

  1. Preparation:

    • Selection of Underwriters: Companies usually hire investment banks or financial institutions to act as underwriters. Underwriters help in determining the IPO price and assist in the issuance process.
    • Due Diligence: The company undergoes a thorough examination of its financial and operational aspects. This is to ensure that the information provided to potential investors is accurate and complete.

  2. Filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC):

    • The company files a registration statement with the SEC, providing details about its financials, operations, and future plans.
    • The SEC reviews the filing to ensure compliance with regulations and to protect investors' interests.

  3. Quiet Period:

    • After filing with the SEC, there is typically a "quiet period" during which the company and underwriters refrain from making public statements about the offering.

  4. Roadshow:

    • The company and underwriters conduct a roadshow to promote the IPO to potential investors. This involves presentations and meetings to showcase the company's potential and attract interest.

  5. Pricing:

    • The underwriters and the company determine the IPO price, which is the price at which the shares will be offered to the public. This is a crucial step, as it influences the amount of capital the company can raise and the valuation of the company.

  6. Allotment and Listing:

    • Once the IPO price is set, shares are allotted to institutional and retail investors based on demand.
    • The company's shares are officially listed on a stock exchange, and trading begins.

  7. Post-IPO:

    • The company is now subject to public scrutiny and regulatory requirements.
    • Shareholders, including employees and early investors, may have the opportunity to sell their shares on the open market.

Key IPO dates include:

Offering Date:

The date on which the company's shares become available for purchase by the public.


Listing Date:

The date on which the shares start trading on the stock exchange.

Closing Date:

The date on which the IPO subscription period ends, and final allotments are determined.
